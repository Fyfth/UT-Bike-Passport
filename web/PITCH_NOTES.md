# UT Bike Passport Pitch Notes

## Slide 1

- Bike theft at UT is not just a theft problem. It is a broken recovery workflow.
- UT Bike Passport gives students one place to register, report, and recover.
- This is the student-facing layer, not a replacement for every UT system.

## Slide 2

- Today the process breaks at the handoff points.
- Before theft, students often do not have a clean ownership record.
- After theft, reporting and recovery are fragmented, which slows everything down.

## Slide 3

- UT may already have separate pages and processes, but students experience them as disconnected.
- Our insight is that the real product is workflow integration.
- That makes this more realistic to adopt and more useful immediately.

## Slide 4

- The wedge is narrow on purpose.
- First create the passport, then mark a bike missing, then route found-bike leads back to the owner.
- That solves a real student pain point without needing campus-wide replacement first.

## Slide 5

- The MVP already works end to end.
- A rider creates a passport, posts the bike missing, a campus helper submits a found lead, and the owner gets notified.
- The missing board adds both visibility for students and pressure for campus response.

## Slide 6

- This can outlast the hackathon because it creates value before and after theft.
- It helps students now and creates a realistic integration path for UT partners later.
- The best framing is workflow layer, not institutional replacement.

## Slide 7

- The stack is practical: Next.js, React, TypeScript, Tailwind, Leaflet, server actions, and a simple data layer.
- The matching engine already uses serial, make, model, color, and keyword overlap.
- Codex helped us move fast across debugging, product logic, UI cleanup, and pitch prep.

## Slide 8

- The recovery loop already works today.
- The right AI roadmap is image comparison between passport photos and found-bike photos.
- Closing line: we are making recovered bikes easier to return, not just easier to report.
