UT Bike Passport

The student workflow for bike ownership, theft reporting, and recovery.

Register Report Recover
From fragmented pages to one recovery path.

Students do not just lose bikes to theft. They lose recovery because the workflow is disconnected.

Problem

The system breaks at the handoff points.

Students deal with separate pages for ownership, theft reporting, and recovery. That delay is what kills recovery.

Before theft
No record

Serial, photos, and proof are scattered or missing.

During theft
Too slow

Students rebuild details when speed matters most.

After recovery
No match

Recovered bikes can still go unclaimed without a clean owner path.

Multiple UT pages. No end-to-end integration.

That is why recovered bikes can still miss their owner and end up outside the student recovery loop.

Why this matters

UT does not need another page. It needs one student-facing workflow.

The goal is not to replace UT systems. The goal is to connect them in a way students can actually use.

Today

Fragmented flow

  • Ownership proof lives in one place or nowhere.
  • Missing reports happen somewhere else.
  • Recovered bikes still need manual routing.
UT Bike Passport

Connected flow

  • Create a passport before theft.
  • Mark the bike missing in one tap.
  • Route found-bike leads back to the owner.
Better student experience Faster recovery loop Realistic adoption path
Solution

One place to register, report, and recover.

The wedge is intentionally narrow: ownership first, recovery second, campus visibility as leverage.

01 | Register

Bike passport

Serial, make, model, photos, lock setup, and parking habits.

02 | Report

Missing mode

Mark the bike missing and place it on the campus board.

03 | Recover

Found-bike desk

Helpers submit leads and the likely owner gets alerted.

This is the wedge.

Start with the workflow students need now. Expand into campus partners later.
